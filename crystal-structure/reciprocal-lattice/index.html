Reciprocal lattice and Brillouin zone

Definition​

Given a (direct) lattice of points R\textbf{R}R, a point G\textbf{G}G is a point in the reciprocal lattice if and only if

e(iG⋅R)=1e^{(i\textbf{G}\cdot\textbf{R})} = 1e(iG⋅R)=1

for all points R\textbf{R}R of the direct lattice.

Construction of reciprocal lattice​

Let us write the points of the direct lattice in the form:

R=n1a1+n2a2+n3a3\textbf{R} = n_1\textbf{a}_1 + n_2 \textbf{a}_2 + n_3\textbf{a}_3R=n1​a1​+n2​a2​+n3​a3​

With n1n_1n1​, n2n_2n2​ and n3n_3n3​ integers, and with a1\textbf{a}_1a1​, a2\textbf{a}_2a2​, and a3\textbf{a}_3a3​ being primitive lattice vectors of the direct lattice. Now we can construct the reciprocal vectors by following this condition:

The primitive lattice vectors of the reciprocal lattice, say b1\textbf{b}_1b1​, b2\textbf{b}_2b2​, and b3\textbf{b}_3b3​ are defined to have the following property:

ai⋅bj=2πδij\textbf{a}_i\cdot\textbf{b}_j = 2\pi\delta_{ij}ai​⋅bj​=2πδij​

We can construct vectors bi\textbf{b}_ibi​ to have the above desired property, as follows:

b1=2π a2×a3a1⋅(a2×a3)\textbf{b}_1 = \frac{2\pi~\textbf{a}_2\times\textbf{a}_3}{\textbf{a}_1\cdot(\textbf{a}_2\times\textbf{a}_3)}b1​=a1​⋅(a2​×a3​)2π a2​×a3​​

We can write any arbitrary point in the reciprocal space as

G=m1b1+m2b2+m3b3\textbf{G} = m_1\textbf{b}_1 + m_2\textbf{b}_2 + m_3\textbf{b}_3G=m1​b1​+m2​b2​+m3​b3​

where m1\textbf{m}_1m1​, m2\textbf{m}_2m2​, and m3\textbf{m}_3m3​ are integers. So the reciprocal lattice is a lattice as well. Reciprocal lattice points can be understood as families of lattice planes of the direct lattice. A lattice plane is plane containing at least three non-colinear points of a lattice. A family of lattice planes is an infinite set of equally separated lattice planes which taken together contain all points of the lattice.

The families of lattice planes are in one-to-one correspondence with the possible directions of reciprocal lattice vectors, to which they are normal. Further the spacing between these lattice planes is d=2π/∣Gmin∣d = 2\pi/\mid\textbf{G}_{min}\midd=2π/∣Gmin​∣, where Gmin\textbf{G}_{min}Gmin​ is the minimum length reciprocal lattice vector in this normal direction.
